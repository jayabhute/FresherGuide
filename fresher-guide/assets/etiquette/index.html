Digital & Hierarchy Etiquette

Remote excellence + organizational respect — keywords for career growth.

Digital / remote (ATS soft skills)

Situation Proper behavior Avoid
Video Calls
  • Turn on camera during meetings (if required)
  • Keep microphone on mute when not speaking
  • Use headset for clear audio
  • Look at camera while speaking
  • Maintain clean and distraction-free background
  • Eating during calls
  • Background noise or disturbance
  • Multitasking or distracted behavior
  • Camera off without reason
Email Communication
  • Reply within 24 hours
  • Use clear subject line
  • Write professional and structured content
  • Use proper signature (Name, Role, Contact)
  • Use bullet points for clarity
  • Using too many emojis
  • No subject line
  • Reply-all unnecessarily
  • Informal or chat-style language
Slack / Teams Communication
  • Use proper threads for conversations
  • Mention only relevant people using @name
  • Write complete and clear messages
  • Keep communication professional
  • Sending only “hi” or “hey” messages
  • Using @channel for unnecessary updates
  • Spamming messages
  • Sending unclear instructions
File Sharing & Documentation
  • Use proper file naming format (ProjectName_Date_Initials)
  • Organize files in structured folders
  • Share only required access/permissions
  • Keep version control properly
  • Random names like “final_final_v2.docx”
  • Sending unorganized files
  • Sharing without permission
  • Confusing file versions
Remote Meetings Etiquette
  • Be on time for online meetings
  • Use headset for clear communication
  • Stay focused and attentive
  • Show engagement (nod, respond when needed)
  • Typing loudly during meetings
  • Eating or multitasking
  • Background interruptions
  • Joining late or leaving early

Hierarchy & respect (promotion mindset)

With whom Proper behavior (metric-driven) Avoid
Senior Leaders
  • Communicate in a concise and structured way
  • Ask 1–2 relevant and insightful questions
  • Show preparation before meetings or discussions
  • Ignoring or avoiding communication
  • Over-sharing personal or unnecessary details
  • Nervous or unprepared behavior
Your Manager
  • Give weekly progress updates
  • Inform early about risks, issues, or delays
  • Ask for feedback regularly (every 2 weeks)
  • Hiding mistakes or delays
  • Last-minute problem reporting
  • Lack of communication
Peers (Colleagues)
  • Collaborate and support teamwork
  • Share credit for group contributions
  • Share useful resources and knowledge
  • Gossip or negative discussions
  • Unfair competition
  • Taking credit for others’ work
Interns / Juniors
  • Provide clear and structured guidance
  • Be patient while explaining tasks
  • Support learning and development
  • Showing arrogance or superiority
  • Ignoring help or doubts
  • Mocking or discouraging juniors
Titles / Names
  • Use Mr./Ms. until invited otherwise
  • Respect designation and hierarchy
  • Use formal communication initially
  • Using nicknames too early
  • First-name usage without permission
  • Informal or casual addressing in office

10 Common Mistakes Freshers Make (ATS-aware)

Dress code confusion – first week observe, when in doubt go one step formal. Reflects personal brand.
2–3 minutes late daily – builds reputation. Aim 10 min early = punctuality metric.
Interrupting – let others finish; note-taking boosts active listening score.
Phone in meetings – face down or away. scrolling reduces perceived professionalism by 40%.
Smelly food at desk – use pantry. Impacts team comfort & collaboration rating.
Forgetting "please/thank you" – two magic words; gratitude increases peer recommendations.
Borrow & forget to return – return pens/chargers same day. Builds reliability KPI.
Gossiping – stay out; trustworthiness drops by 70% when caught gossiping.
Missing deadlines without warning – communicate 48h in advance: "risk flagged early".
Oversharing personal life – maintain boundaries; focus on professional contributions.
