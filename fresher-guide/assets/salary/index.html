Fresher Negotiation Guide

Negotiation Strategy

  • Always give a range: "I'm looking at ₹3.8L to ₹4.5L, depending on the complete package."
  • Highlight your strengths: Certifications, internships, projects, GitHub portfolio
  • Ask about variable pay: "What was the average variable payout in the last year?"
  • Request breakup: "Could you share the fixed and variable component breakup?"
  • Take time: "Thank you, can I have a day to review the offer?"

Exact Phrases to Use

  • "Based on my research and industry standards for freshers, I was expecting around ₹4L to ₹4.5L. Is there flexibility in the fixed component?"
  • "I'm excited about this role. Could you explain the variable pay structure - is it on target or actual performance?"
  • "What is the breakup between basic, HRA, and special allowance? I want to understand the tax implications."
  • "Is there any joining/relocation bonus? Are there any bonds or service agreements?"

Power Moves (Fresher Special)

  • Mention competing offers: If you have genuine offers, mention them politely
  • Ask for joining bonus: Instead of base hike, request one-time joining bonus
  • Request early review: Ask for 6-month performance review instead of annual
  • Learning budget: Inquire about certification/training budget
  • Stock options: For startups, ask about ESOPs or equity

Before You Sign - Complete Checklist

  • Probation Period: Confirm duration & salary during probation
  • Annual Bonus: Ask about bonus cycle and typical payout
  • PF & Gratuity: Check if included in CTC calculation
  • Notice Period: Duration and buyback policy
  • Bond/Agreement: Service agreement or penalty for early exit
  • Working Hours: Flexibility, overtime policy
  • Leave Policy: Annual leaves, sick leaves, holidays
  • Health Insurance: Coverage for self and family

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Single number mistake: Never state a single fixed number too early
  • Ignoring variable pay: Don't assume you'll get 100% variable
  • Skipping bond details: Always read agreement carefully
  • Not reading offer letter: Never sign without reading thoroughly
  • Accepting first offer: Always negotiate, even if it's good
